# Crypt Cards

## Summary
Crypt cards represent the vampires a Methuselah hopes to control. They form the **crypt** — one of a Methuselah's two decks — with amber marble backs and oval portraits on the front [src-001]. A crypt must contain **at least 12** cards (no upper limit).

## Anatomy of a Crypt Card
Each crypt card has the following elements [src-001]:

### Name
Each vampire is unique — only one copy of a given vampire can be in play at a time. If two Methuselahs try to control the same vampire, it becomes a **contested card** (see ruling on Contested Cards, future page).

### Blood Capacity
The number in the **red circle, lower right** of the card. Represents:
- The vampire's relative **age** (higher = older).
- The amount of pool a Methuselah must invest to bring the vampire into play (through influence).
- The **maximum blood** the vampire can hold. Excess blood drains back to the blood bank immediately.
- A soft cap on how many blood-costed cards they can play or wounds they can mend.

An **uncontrolled** vampire in your uncontrolled region stacks pool counters on it as you invest. When those counters ≥ capacity, you can reveal the vampire and move them to the ready region during an influence phase. Those counters become their starting blood; any excess drains back to the bank [src-001].

### Clan
Symbol at the top of the attribute bar (left marble stripe). Each vampire belongs to one clan. Some library cards require a member of a specific clan to play; others affect only vampires of a specific clan. See the Quick Reference section of the rulebook for clan symbols (src-001 p. 53) — clan pages TBD.

### Disciplines
Supernatural powers shown as symbols at the bottom of the attribute bar [src-001]:
- **Square symbol** — vampire has the **basic** level of that Discipline. Only the basic (plain text) effect of a card that requires this Discipline can be used.
- **Diamond symbol** — vampire has the **superior** level. They may use either the basic or the superior (bold text) effect — **not both**.

Library cards with a Discipline requirement can only be played by a vampire who has that Discipline at the required level. Some library cards list multiple Disciplines as alternatives; see [[library#Requirements for Playing Cards]].

### Group (Advanced)
Number above the upper-left corner of the text box. A Methuselah's crypt must be built from vampires of a **single group or two consecutive groups**. This restricts deckbuilding, not in-game acquisition — you can still steal vampires from any group through play [src-001].

### Advanced Icon (Advanced)
Some vampires have an **Advanced** icon under the clan icon, marking an *advanced* version of the same vampire. Mechanically it is a regular crypt card in all respects [src-001].

### Text Box
Contains:
- Vampire's **sect** (e.g., Camarilla, Sabbat, Anarch, Independent) — placeholder for the sect page.
- **Title** (if any) — influences voting weight in referendums.
- Special abilities or other attributes (text).

### Expansion Symbol & Illustration
Expansion set this printing came from (no game effect); illustrator credit. Copyright line cites Paradox Interactive AB [src-001].

## Imbued (advanced)
Some expansions include other crypt-card types. **Imbued** (mortal protagonists) are the main alternative. Full rules live in the Appendix: [Imbued Rules](https://www.vekn.net/rulebook/appendix-imbued-rules). Not yet covered by this wiki — ingest when relevant.
