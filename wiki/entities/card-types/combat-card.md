# Combat Cards

## Summary
A **combat card** is a library minion card played by a minion **during combat** [src-001]. This page covers the *card type*. Combat **mechanics** (strike sequence, rounds, damage, press, maneuver, dodge, etc.) arrive in stage 4 ingest — see future `[[combat]]` ruling.

## Core Rules (card-level only)
- Combat cards are only playable inside a combat — i.e., after a block has resolved successfully and combat has begun (see `[[combat]]`).
- The acting (striking) minion and the opposing minion both can play combat cards during their respective impulse windows.
- Cost and Discipline requirements apply as with any library card — see [[library#Requirements for Playing Cards]].

## Sub-roles within combat
Combat cards fulfil various roles (strike, maneuver, press, combat end, damage prevention, combat modifier). These role distinctions are printed in card text, not on the attribute bar. They belong in the `[[combat]]` ruling once we ingest combat in stage 4.

## Cancellation
> If a combat card used for a strike is cancelled, the minion who played it must choose a strike which may come from another strike card. [src-001]

In other words: cancelling a strike card forces the attacker to pick a replacement strike — either a default strike (e.g., hand strike) or another strike card in hand. Cost of the cancelled strike is still paid per the non-action cancel rule [src-001].
