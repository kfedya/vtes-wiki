# Library Cards

## Summary
Library cards represent the wider World of Darkness — allies, equipment, actions, tactical combat responses, supernatural abilities. Green marble backs, square illustrations [src-001]. A library is **60–90** cards.

## Top-Level Categories
Library cards split into two broad categories based on who plays them [src-001]:

### Master Cards
Played by the **Methuselah** directly. No icon at the top of the attribute bar. Played during the **master phase** as master phase actions (with out-of-turn exceptions). Full page: [[master]].

### Minion Cards
Played by the **minions** (vampires or allies) the Methuselah controls. Always have an icon at the top of the attribute bar indicating the specific minion-card type [src-001]. A minion card may belong to **multiple** types at once (e.g., an action + action modifier).

Minion card sub-types covered by this wiki:
- [[action]] — performed as the minion's action for the turn.
- [[action-modifier]] — tweak an in-progress action.
- [[reaction]] — respond to another Methuselah's action.
- [[combat-card]] — used inside combat (strikes, maneuvers, etc.).
- [[political-action]] — call a referendum as an action (vampires only).
- [[ally]] — put a non-vampire minion into play.
- [[equipment]] — attach to the acting minion.
- [[retainer]] — attach to the acting minion, has its own life.

### Advanced Minion Cards
- **Reflex** (Advanced) — cancels a specified kind of card "as it is played" [src-001]. Separate page TBD.
- **Event** (Advanced) — played during the **discard phase** using a discard-phase action, affects the World of Darkness globally. Each event can only be played once per game; controlled by the player who played it [src-001]. Separate page TBD.

## Playing a Card
> A card is played by announcing its effects, showing the card and placing it from the hand in the ash heap upon resolution. [src-001]

Some cards are instead placed **in play** (locations, equipment, retainers, allies) and remain there until burned. Action cards are **temporarily out of play** between play and resolution — they do not reach the ash heap until they resolve or are cancelled.

### "As It Is Played"
Only two things can act on a card during the narrow "as played" window before resolution: **reflex cards** that cancel, and **wake effects**. Even the replacement draw happens after this window [src-001].

## Drawing Cards
> Whenever you play a card from your hand, you draw another from your library to replace it (unless card text says otherwise). [src-001]

- Starting hand size = **7**.
- If your library is empty, you stop drawing but keep playing.
- Effects that change hand size (up or down) resolve immediately — discard down or draw up to match.
- Effects that replace cards later effectively reduce hand size for the duration of the effect.

## Requirements for Playing Cards
A library card's attribute bar shows any of:
- **Card type** (except for master cards).
- **Clan** required.
- **Discipline** required — the acting minion must possess it at the listed level (see [[crypt#Disciplines]]).
- **Cost** — blood (red drop) or pool (white diamond with skull).

Some cards add text-based requirements like **capacity** or **title** [src-001].

### Multi-Discipline Cards
Some cards list several Disciplines as alternatives — the vampire needs one. Other effects on the card may require a specific listed Discipline or require possession of several [src-001].

### Costs
- **Blood cost** (red drop) — the vampire burns that much of its own blood.
- **Pool cost** (white diamond with skull) — the Methuselah burns that much of their own pool.
- Costs can only be paid with the Methuselah's or minion's **own resources**. Alt-costs (e.g., "burn the Edge", "discard a card") must also use the payer's own Edge or hand [src-001].
- Minions have access to their controller's resources (e.g., a minion can discard from its controller's hand to pay an alt-cost).

### Optional Cost Reducers
Can be applied when the card is played (to meet the cost) or upon resolution (wait to see if the action succeeds first). If the card is cancelled, the reducer isn't used [src-001].

## Targeting
If a card targets another card, that target must be **in play** (controlled) [src-001]:
- Vampires in **torpor** are eligible targets by default.
- Vampires in the **uncontrolled region** are **not** eligible.
- **Contested cards** are **not** eligible.
