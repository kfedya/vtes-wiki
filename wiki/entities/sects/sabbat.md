# Sabbat (Legacy)

## Summary
The **Sabbat** is a legacy vampire sect from sets prior to VTES Fifth Edition. Only Sabbat vampires can hold the Sabbat titles: **bishop**, **archbishop**, **priscus**, **cardinal**, and **regent**. The **Prisci Block** is a sub-referendum mechanism unique to the Sabbat [src-001 p. 40].

> **Legacy Note:** The rules for "Other Vampire Sects" — Sabbat and Laibon — relate to cards released in sets prior to VTES Fifth Edition. These sets are fully compatible with 5E, but the stories are in the past of the World of Darkness [src-001 p. 40].

## Titles and Votes
During the polling step of a referendum, a Methuselah gains [src-001 p. 40]:
- **1 vote** for each ready **bishop**
- **2 votes** for each ready **archbishop**
- **3 votes** for each ready **cardinal**
- **4 votes** for the ready **regent**

## Uniqueness and Contests
- The title of **regent** is **unique** [src-001 p. 40].
- The title of **archbishop** is associated with a particular **city** and can be contested by another vampire who claims any title to the same city [src-001 p. 40] — cross-sect contest analogous to the Camarilla prince rule.
- The other Sabbat titles — **bishop**, **priscus**, **cardinal** — are **not unique** and **cannot be contested** [src-001 p. 40].

## The Prisci Block
The prisci as a group have **3 votes** in a referendum. These 3 votes are cast for or against according to a **sub-referendum** among the prisci [src-001 p. 40]:

1. Each ready priscus provides **1 ballot** for the sub-referendum; **votes may not** be used in this sub-referendum.
2. Each ballot is either "for" or "against" the **main** referendum.
3. Whichever side has the greater number of ballots in the sub-referendum gains the **3 votes** in the main referendum.
4. If the sub-referendum is **tied**, the prisci block **abstains** from the main referendum.

### Dynamic Prisci Block
As vampires cast their ballots in the sub-referendum, the prisci block of 3 votes may **shift** between "for", "against", and "abstain" as the sub-referendum's majority changes [src-001 p. 40].

A vampire must cast **all of their votes and ballots in agreement** [src-001 p. 40].

## Antitribu Distinction
For the purpose of these rules, the **antitribu** clans are **distinct** from their non-antitribu counterparts. A vampire of an antitribu clan does **not** qualify to play a card that requires the non-antitribu counterpart, and vice versa. Likewise, if a vampire changes sect, their clan does **not** automatically change [src-001 p. 40].

### Examples
- A Malkavian who becomes Sabbat via *Into the Fire* is still a **Malkavian**, not a Malkavian antitribu [src-001 p. 40].
- A Lasombra who becomes Camarilla is a **Camarilla Lasombra** [src-001 p. 40].
