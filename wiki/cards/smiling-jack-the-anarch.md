# Smiling Jack, The Anarch

> Card text pending krcg.org ingest.

— [krcg.org entry](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Smiling%20Jack%2C%20The%20Anarch)

## Why it has a page
Smiling Jack forces **mandatory** pool/blood payment even when it would oust the controller — a rare counter-intuitive edge case that governs game-ending turns. It also has the unusual "mix blood and pool" allowance across multiple vampires.

## Rulings

### Mandatory even if it ousts
- If you control Smiling Jack at your **unlock phase**, you must move 1 pool onto the card **even if doing so ousts you** [src-001 p. 51]. No opt-out.

### Other Methuselahs' burn cost
- Each other Methuselah must **burn 1 pool or 1 vampire blood per counter on Jack** [src-001 p. 51].
- **Mix is allowed:** the burn can be split across multiple vampires and the pool in any combination [src-001 p. 51].
- **Failing to burn 1 blood from an empty vampire does not lessen the obligation** [src-001 p. 51] — if you designated a vampire with 0 blood, you still owe the burn elsewhere.

## Interactions
- **With [[ending-the-game]]:** a controller being ousted by their own Smiling Jack counts for VP purposes like any other ouster — the prey (of the self-ousted controller) gains 1 VP and 6 pool from the prey slot.
- **With [[torpor]]:** vampires in torpor are **controlled** but not ready; they still count for the "burn 1 blood" pool-substitute on Smiling Jack if their controller chooses them.
- **With [[unlock-phase]]:** Smiling Jack's trigger is specifically in the unlock phase of the controller.

## Common Mistakes
- Trying to skip Smiling Jack's 1-pool payment when at 1 pool — the rule forces the payment even if it ousts you.
- Assuming "1 blood from each other Methuselah" — it's 1 pool *or* 1 blood, and it's per counter.
- Designating an empty vampire as the blood-source and claiming the obligation is satisfied — no, you still owe the pool or find blood elsewhere.
