# World of Darkness Glossary

Lore-layer terminology used across *Vampire: The Eternal Struggle* and the broader *Vampire: The Masquerade* setting. Rules-layer meanings of overlapping terms live on their respective ruling pages (e.g., [[blood-hunt]] for the in-game referendum mechanic) — this page is the **setting** reference.

All entries are paraphrased from the rulebook's World of Darkness Glossary [src-001 p. 45] except where quoted verbatim.

## Factions & Sects

### The Camarilla
A sect made up of several clans that have banded together and are governed by the **Traditions**. The Camarilla's ruling body is the **Inner Circle**. Represents the "establishment" vampire society — secrecy, order, elder hierarchy. Rules overview: [[entities/sects/camarilla]].

### The Sabbat
A violent sect of vampires bent on destroying the **Antediluvians**. Rejects the **Masquerade** and the Traditions. Rules overview: [[entities/sects/sabbat]].

### Anarchs
A sect of vampires who fall outside of the Camarilla and rebel against its authority. Rules overview: [[entities/sects/anarch]].

### Sect
A group of Kindred arguably united under a common philosophy. In VTES the primary sects are the Camarilla, Sabbat, Anarch, Laibon, and independents — see [[politics]] and sect pages.

### Clan
A group of vampires that share certain mystical and physical characteristics. In 5E the five "core" clans are Malkavian, Nosferatu, Toreador, Tremere, and Ventrue — see [[quick-reference#clans]].

## Vampire Hierarchy

### Caine
The **first vampire**, from whom all other vampires are allegedly descended. The mythic ancestor of all Kindred.

### Antediluvian
An ancient vampire, a grandchilde of Caine. Most of the major clans of the Camarilla were created by the Antediluvians. The Sabbat's stated goal is to destroy them.

### Methuselah
A powerful vampire, thousands of years old (but still a few generations younger than the Antediluvians), involved in the Eternal Struggle from afar or in complete anonymity. **In VTES, each player represents a Methuselah** — your vampires are your pawns.

### Kindred
The term that vampires use to refer collectively to their kind. **Sabbat vampires scorn the term** (they prefer others or none).

### Inner Circle
The group of vampires who comprise the ruling body of the Camarilla. Highest Camarilla title in VTES — see [[voting#camarilla-titles]].

### Justicar
A vampire who serves as **judge, jury and executioner** of Camarilla vampires who have broken the Traditions.

### Archon
A powerful vampire who travels from city to city, in the service of a justicar.

### Prince
A Camarilla vampire who rules a city and enforces the Traditions upon the city's vampire population.

### Primogen
A council of vampires in a city that supports the city's ruling prince.

### Baron
An Anarch ruler of a domain. See [[entities/sects/anarch]].

## Mortal World

### Kine
A term for mortals, **largely contemptuous**.

### Canaille
Mortals, especially referring to the most unsavory elements of mortal society.

### Ghoul
A mortal who drinks the blood of a vampire but has not been drained beforehand. A servant of sorts; distinct from an **Embraced** vampire.

### Retainer
A mortal or creature that serves a vampire master. In rules terms: a retainer card — see [[retainer]].

## Vampiric Practices

### Embrace
The act of transforming a mortal into a vampire — creating new Kindred.

### Amaranth
**The act of killing a vampire by drinking all of their blood.** Commonly known as **diablerie**. Rules resolution: [[diablerie]].

### Blood Bond
A mysterious link that forms between Kindred that drink one another's blood. The blood bond can give the donor control of the recipient.

### Blood Hunt
A system of punishment for vampires who disregard the laws of the Masquerade. The hunted vampire is slain by those who heed the call. In VTES, the blood hunt **referendum** follows a diablerie — see [[blood-hunt]].

### Vitae
**Blood of a vampire.** Specifically vampiric blood (as opposed to mortal blood).

### The Beast
The drives and desires that pull Kindred away from their humanity and turn them into monsters. The internal enemy every vampire struggles with.

## Laws & Customs

### The Traditions
**The six laws of the Camarilla.** These Traditions are laws that protect the vampires from mortals and from one another. The Camarilla justicars enforce them; the prince enforces them locally.

### The Masquerade
The Tradition of **keeping mortals ignorant of the existence of vampires**, essential to survival. The signature Camarilla law — the Sabbat reject it.

### Praxis
The right of a prince to rule a domain. Challenges to praxis are how cities change hands.

### Domain
The fiefdom (usually a city) claimed by a vampire, most often a prince. Anarch **barons** also hold domains.

### Elysium
The name given to a place where Camarilla elders meet. Violence within Elysium is forbidden by convention.

### Haven
A vampire's **"home"**; where they find sanctuary from the sun.

### Consanguineous
Belonging to the same clan (usually used to refer to a younger member). Used e.g. in card names like [Consanguineous Boon](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Consanguineous%20Boon).

## Apocalyptic Lore

### Gehenna
The impending Armageddon in which the Antediluvians will rise up and devour all Kindred. The existential threat that motivates the Sabbat and haunts the elders.

### The Book of Nod
The sacred book of the Kindred, which allegedly traces their origins and history. **Most of it has been lost to time.**
