# VEKN Tournament Rules — Overview

Quick-reference index for the VEKN Tournament Rules document, version updated 21 September 2025 [src-032]. Original at <https://www.vekn.net/tournament-rules>.

The VEKN Tournament Rules govern sanctioned play. They sit on top of the basic game rules in the [VTES Fifth Edition Rulebook](../rulings/game-overview.md) (src-001) — wherever a game-rule and a tournament-rule disagree, **the tournament rule wins for sanctioned events**. The most important divergence: a withdrawing Methuselah scores **0.5 VP**, not 1 (see [[withdraw]] and [[scoring]]).

## Headline rules at a glance [src-032]

| Topic | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Minimum rounds | 3 (preliminaries + final) |
| Round time limit | min 2 hours; final may be longer at judge's option |
| End-of-round | game ends with current minion action, or at end of current phase if notification is outside minion phase |
| Table size | 5 players preferred, 4 if remainder; 6/7/11-player events may use sit-out arrangements |
| Library size (Standard Constructed) | 60 min, 90 max |
| Crypt size (Standard Constructed) | 12 min |
| Game Win | ≥ 2 VP **and** strictly more VP than every other player at the table |
| Victory Point — oust prey | 1 VP |
| Victory Point — survive at time | 0.5 VP (full 1 VP if you are the sole survivor) |
| Victory Point — withdraw | **0.5 VP** (overrides the basic-rules 1 VP) |
| Tournament Points (5-player) | 60 / 48 / 36 / 24 / 12 |
| Tournament Points (4-player) | 60 / 48 / **bye = 36** / 24 / 12 (no third place — "table bye") |
| Tie-breaker chain | Game Wins → Victory Points → Tournament Points → fair random |
| Final-round seating | index-card placement, lowest qualifier first, then alternating ends or insertions |
| Final tournament winner | highest VP **in the final only**; tie-breaker = preliminary ranking |

## Tournament organization

- **Sanctioning deadline** — apply ≥ 28 days before the event [src-032 §8.1]; advertise ≥ 28 days ahead [src-032 §8.2].
- **Event reporting** — due within 8 days; "Not Received" 9–14 days; "Delinquent" 15–30 days; **invalid after 30 days** (results don't count toward ratings) [src-032 §9.2–9.4].
- **Multi-Judge System** — allowed at non-qualifier, non-championship events with ≥ 8 participants; lets judges/organizers play; identifies 6 judges in priority order [src-032 §2.9].
- **Multi-Deck System** — allowed at non-NC / non-GP / non-CC events; players may freely switch decks between rounds; finals use either *Best-Performing Deck* (highest single-round score by GW → VP → TP, random among ties) or *Free Choice* — TO announces method before round 1 [src-032 §3.1.5–3.1.5a].
- **Proxy cards** (§4.5) — TO may permit at non-NC / non-GP / non-CC events; no quantity limit; colour copies on standard paper, illustrator name legible, in opaque sleeves in front of a regular VTES/Jyhad card; **custom artwork or text not allowed unless representing a game token** (e.g., The Embrace).

## Bowing out, tardiness, in-game breaks

- Player who fails to appear within 15 minutes of round start receives a **Loss** for the round [src-032 §3.3].
- Player who quits an event before the next round's seatings are generated is dropped cleanly; quitting after seatings are generated takes a **Loss** for the upcoming round and is then dropped [src-032 §3.1.4 / §3.3.2].
- Mid-game personal break must be cleared by a judge, who may extend the table's time limit [src-032 §3.3.3].

## Pre-game procedure [src-032 §3.2 / §3.4]

1. Players shuffle their decks.
2. Players present decks to their **predator** for additional shuffling and/or cutting (a judge may shuffle instead, at judge discretion).
3. After the predator returns the deck, the player may make one final cut.
4. Each player draws **7 library cards** and **4 crypt cards**.

The game is considered to have **begun** once all players have presented their decks to their predators [src-032 Appendix B — *Game Begins*].

## Set restrictions and "current text wins" [src-032 §6.1.1]

A TO may restrict an event to specific card sets, but in any constructed event **the most recent printing's text is what's legal** even if a card was reprinted in a set the TO excluded. Example given by VEKN: in a base-set-only event, *Misdirection* still locks only one minion (per its current text), not the original wording.

Tournaments run with set restrictions are **not** included in VEKN player ratings.

## V:EKN numbers and ratings [src-032 §9.6 / Appendix A]

Every participant must have a unique VEKN membership number; results filed under temporary numbers, names, or placeholders are not counted toward ratings. The VEKN publishes ratings grouped by **Standard Constructed** and **Limited** (all formats merged) — see <https://www.vekn.net/rating-system>.
