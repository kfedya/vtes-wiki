# V5 Constructed Format

The **V5 format** is a Standard Constructed variant with a **reduced card pool**, intended to let new player groups and local game shops experiment with deck-building inside the current World of Darkness setting [src-032 §6.4]. It does **not** replace Standard Constructed, which remains the format used for major VEKN-sanctioned events (Continental Championships, National Championships, Grand Prix).

Mechanics-wise V5 is identical to Standard Constructed: same library 60–90 / crypt ≥ 12 sizes, same scoring, same banned list (see [[banned-cards]]).

## Print-version clarification [src-032 §6.4]

> Any Vampire: The Eternal Struggle legal print version of the above cards is legal.

VEKN's own examples:
- A Latin-language *Govern the Unaligned* from the Malkavian Fifth Edition New Blood — **legal**.
- A *Govern the Unaligned* from the 1994 *Jyhad* base set — **also legal**, because the *card* exists in the V5 pool, even though the printing comes from a set outside the pool.

This is consistent with Standard Constructed's "current text wins" principle [src-032 §6.1.1] — the print may be old, but the card text used at the table is the current text.

## What this format excludes

Anything not listed above. Notable absences as of 2025-09-21:
- Pre-V5 expansions and base sets beyond the explicitly listed promos / Fall of London / Shadows of Berlin / 30th Anniversary.
- Older promos not part of Promo Pack 3 (Icons) or Promo Pack 4 (Rebels).
- Storyline-event reward cards.
- Most legacy bloodlines and disciplines that have no V5 print equivalent (e.g., the legacy Sabbat clans before the 2025-10-26 Sabbat V5 release closed that gap).

If a card you want to play isn't in any release in the list above, it isn't legal in V5 — even if it's legal in Standard Constructed.

## Why this matters at a local table

For a casual playgroup the practical effect of running a V5-only night is: **only V5-shaped decks are possible**. That means no legacy combos that depend on cards from Anarchs Unbound, Lords of the Night, Black Hand, Heirs to the Blood, Twilight Rebellion, etc., even if those cards aren't on the [[banned-cards|banned list]]. The format is a self-contained sandbox for the V5-era cardpool, not a "newest rules with all cards" mode.
