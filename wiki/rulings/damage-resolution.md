# Damage Resolution

## Summary
Damage resolution has two ordered steps: **Prevent damage**, then **Mend damage**. Unprevented damage is **successfully inflicted**; vampires burn blood to mend, allies and retainers burn life to absorb. A vampire who cannot mend all their damage is **wounded** and goes to **torpor** (see [[torpor]]) [src-001 p. 31].

## Order
1. **Prevent**: the minion taking damage can play damage-prevention combat cards one at a time until all damage is prevented or the minion chooses to stop [src-001 p. 31].
2. **Mend**: any unprevented damage is successfully inflicted and is then mended (vampires) or absorbed (allies/retainers).

## Vampires (Normal Damage)
- For each point successfully inflicted, the vampire burns **1 blood** to mend [src-001 p. 31].
- A vampire may burn **all** their blood if needed; doing so has no other negative effect.
- Unmended damage leaves the vampire **wounded**, which sends them to **torpor** after all remaining damage is handled [src-001 p. 31].

## Allies and Retainers
- Each point of damage burns **1 life counter**. An ally or retainer reduced to zero life counters is **burned** [src-001 p. 31].
- Allies and retainers treat **aggravated damage the same as normal damage** — 1 life per point [src-001 p. 32 Advanced].

## Retainers in Combat — Targeting
- An attacker may target the opposing minion's **retainer** with a strike, **only at long range**, and must announce the target when the strike is announced [src-001 p. 33].
- **Dodge does not protect a retainer** [src-001 p. 33].

## Aggravated Damage
Aggravated damage (from fire, sunlight, some supernatural sources, and some vampires) differs from normal damage in two ways [src-001 pp. 31–32]:

1. **Cannot be mended.**
   - A vampire does not burn blood to mend aggravated damage; instead, they become **wounded** (go straight to torpor), unless the damage is prevented.

2. **Can burn a wounded vampire outright.**
   - A **wounded** vampire is one that has taken unmendable damage, or one in torpor / on their way to torpor.
   - For each aggravated point inflicted on a wounded vampire, they must burn **1 blood** to prevent destruction. If they cannot, they are **burned** [src-001 p. 32].

### Resolution Order When Both Normal & Aggravated Are Inflicted Simultaneously
- Normal damage is handled **first**, then aggravated [src-001 p. 32].
- This only applies to **unprevented** damage — damage prevention can be used to prevent aggravated before normal.
- If aggravated burns a wounded vampire, they go **straight to the ash heap** — they do **not** pass through torpor first [src-001 p. 32].

### Examples (from rulebook, p. 32)
- **Nassir** — ready, 1 blood, takes 1 aggravated. Cannot mend aggravated. He is wounded and goes to torpor with 1 blood.
- **Tamoszius** — ready, 2 blood, takes 3 aggravated. Becomes wounded by 1 point; for the other 2 points he must burn 2 blood (1 per point) to prevent destruction. Ends empty and in torpor.
- **Ryan** — ready, 1 blood, takes 2 normal + 1 aggravated. Burns 1 blood to mend one normal point. No blood to mend the second normal — becomes wounded. The aggravated point then burns him outright, since he is already wounded and can't burn 1 blood to prevent destruction.

## Environmental Damage
Any damage not inflicted by a minion as a strike or by an explicit effect ("this minion inflicts…") is **environmental** [src-001 p. 31].

- Can have no source (e.g., [Carrion Crows](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Carrion%20Crows)) or a **non-minion** source (e.g., a retainer — [Murder of Crows](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Murder%20of%20Crows)) [src-001 pp. 31, 49].
- **Cannot be dodged** — dodge only protects from the opponent's strike [src-001 pp. 31, 48].
- **Combat ends** ends combat before environmental damage from a strike-timed source (e.g., Carrion Crows) is inflicted [src-001 p. 48].

## Immune to Damage
If a minion is **immune** to damage from a source, any unprevented damage from that source is inflicted **unsuccessfully** [src-001 p. 32 Advanced]:
- The minion does **not** burn blood or life to mend.
- The minion does **not** become wounded because of that damage.

## Fame-Burning Interaction (Aggravated Edge Case)
If a vampire bearing [Fame](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Fame) is **burned** outright by aggravated damage (i.e., they never reach torpor), their controller does **not** burn 3 pool. The 3-pool penalty applies only after the vampire goes to torpor [src-001 p. 49].

## Common Cards Involved
- [Carrion Crows](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Carrion%20Crows) — environmental, strike-timed, not dodgeable, beaten by combat ends [src-001 p. 48].
- [Murder of Crows](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Murder%20of%20Crows) — retainer-sourced environmental damage [src-001 p. 49].
- [Fame](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Fame) — 3-pool when vampire goes to torpor, not when aggravated-burned directly [src-001 p. 49].
- [Hidden Strength](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Hidden%20Strength) — damage prevention; requires damage to prevent [src-001 p. 49].

## Common Mistakes
- Trying to dodge environmental damage.
- Handling aggravated before normal when both hit simultaneously (normal comes first on unprevented damage).
- Sending an aggravated-burned wounded vampire through torpor.
- Giving pool to Fame's controller when the aggravated burn skipped torpor.
- Forgetting that allies/retainers treat aggravated the same as normal.
