# Maneuver

## Summary
A **maneuver** changes combat range between **close** (default) and **long**. Maneuvers are played during the Determine Range step of each round. Combatants may trade maneuvers back and forth; a minion cannot play two maneuvers in a row, and a minion has no maneuvers by default [src-001 p. 29].

## Range in Combat
- Each round starts at **close** unless a maneuver changes it [src-001 p. 29].
- The range determines which strikes and which strike-resolution effects are available:
  - Most strikes are close only.
  - **Ranged** strikes, strikes doing **R** damage, and defensive strikes (**dodge**, **combat ends**) work at any range [src-001 p. 30].

## How Maneuvers Are Gained
A minion has zero maneuvers by default. They must use a card or other effect to gain a maneuver [src-001 p. 29]. Sources include:
- Combat cards that grant a maneuver.
- Weapons or other cards in play.
- Strike cards that grant a maneuver — but see the strike-card commitment rule below.

## Ping-Pong Rule
Combatants can continue trading maneuvers for as long as they wish to offset the opponent's last change. However:
- A minion **cannot play two maneuvers in a row** (which would effectively cancel each other) [src-001 p. 29].

## Strike-Card / Weapon Maneuver Commitment
When a maneuver comes from a **strike card or a weapon**, the minion is **choosing their strike as well** — playing the strike card during the maneuver step instead of during the choose-strike step. Consequences:
- They **cannot use another strike card or weapon to maneuver again in the same round** [src-001 p. 29].
- They are committed to that strike. If the strike is later cancelled (e.g., by [Immortal Grapple](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Immortal%20Grapple)), the minion **cannot strike at all** this round — they do **not** get to choose another strike [src-001 p. 47, [.44 Magnum](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=.44%20Magnum)].

## "Optional" Maneuvers
If a combat card gives a maneuver as part of an effect, the maneuver **must** be used that round of combat. If the effect says "optional maneuver", the minion may decline [src-001 p. 29].

## Single-Round Scope
A maneuver granted by a card applies only to the round it is played. It cannot be saved for a later round. Cards that grant a maneuver AND a press (e.g., [Resist Earth's Grasp](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Resist%20Earth%27s%20Grasp)) must use **both** the same round [src-001 p. 50].

## Interaction with Effects That Lock Maneuvers Out
- [Immortal Grapple](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Immortal%20Grapple) must be played **after all maneuvers are done**, not before [src-001 p. 49].

## Example (from rulebook)
Ayelech has blocked Colette; combat begins. Ayelech has a .44 Magnum (2R damage, optional maneuver). Colette (acting) declines to maneuver. Ayelech uses the gun's maneuver to go long — where Colette's hand strike cannot reach. Colette plays a maneuver card to return to close. Ayelech plays another maneuver card back to long. Colette has no more maneuvers, so the range is set to long [src-001 p. 29].

## Common Cards Involved
- [.44 Magnum](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=.44%20Magnum) — gun with optional maneuver; commits to 2R strike if maneuver is used [src-001 p. 47].
- [Immortal Grapple](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Immortal%20Grapple) — after maneuver step; restricts round to hand strikes [src-001 p. 49].
- [Resist Earth's Grasp](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Resist%20Earth%27s%20Grasp) — maneuver + press tied to same round [src-001 p. 50].

## Common Mistakes
- Playing two maneuvers in a row with the same minion.
- Maneuvering with a weapon or strike card, then trying to pick a different strike.
- Carrying a maneuver to a later round.
- Playing Immortal Grapple while maneuvers are still being traded.
