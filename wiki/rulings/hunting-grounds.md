# Hunting Grounds

## Summary
A **hunting ground** is a unique master card, typically clan- or sect-locked, that gives blood to a ready vampire of its associated clan during the unlock phase. The 5E rulebook FAQ describes the Academic Hunting Ground template; it applies to every hunting ground in the set [src-001].

The hunting grounds in the 5E core: Academic (Tremere), Asylum (Malkavian), Slum (Nosferatu), Society (Toreador), Uptown (Ventrue). Non-hunting-ground locations like [[wiki/cards/wasserschloss-anif-austria.md|Wasserschloss Anif, Austria]] are covered separately below.

## Core Rules (Academic Hunting Ground template) [src-001 p. 47]
- **Requires a ready vampire of the matching clan** to be played. For Academic: a ready Tremere.
- **Any ready vampire you control can receive the blood** — even of another clan. The clan restriction is only on the play requirement, not on the recipient.
- Played during the **master phase**, so you must wait **until next turn** to start gaining blood from it (the first unlock-phase trigger is the turn after it's played).
- Gaining blood from it is **optional**. If you start your unlock phase, declare "no hunting ground blood this turn," and move on to play a master card without gaining blood, that's permitted — you're effectively skipping the trigger.

Examples of hunting grounds that all follow this template [src-001 p. 51]:
- [Academic Hunting Ground](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Academic%20Hunting%20Ground) — Tremere.
- [Asylum Hunting Ground](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Asylum%20Hunting%20Ground) — Malkavian.
- [Slum Hunting Ground](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Slum%20Hunting%20Ground) — Nosferatu.
- [Society Hunting Ground](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Society%20Hunting%20Ground) — Toreador.
- [Uptown Hunting Ground](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Uptown%20Hunting%20Ground) — Ventrue.

## Arcane Library / Art Museum
[src-001 p. 47] — blood-producing locations on the same timing principle:
- Blood added to the receiving vampire comes from the **blood bank**, not from any Methuselah's pool.
- You can spend **2 transfers** during the influence phase to move that blood to your pool afterward.
- Art Museum follows the same rules as Arcane Library.

These are not clan-locked, but they share the optional-gain and master-phase timing rules of hunting grounds.

## Wasserschloss Anif, Austria (Tremere-specific)
[src-001 p. 52] — blood-storage card:
- Can only **receive blood from one Tremere** on any given turn.
- All counters on the card are **lost** if the card is burned.

## Common Mistakes
- Using a hunting ground on the turn you played it — you cannot; must wait until next unlock phase.
- Restricting the recipient to the clan printed on the card — any of your ready vampires can receive the blood.
- Failing to announce "no blood this turn" before moving to another master-phase action — mechanically it doesn't matter (gaining is optional) but be clear at the table.
