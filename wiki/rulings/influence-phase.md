# Influence Phase

## Summary
The **influence phase** is the fourth phase of a turn. You spend **transfers** to move pool onto uncontrolled vampires, pull blood back off them, and bring new crypt cards into your uncontrolled region. A vampire moves to your ready region the moment they have at least as many blood counters as their capacity [src-001 pp. 35–36].

## Transfers
- Each Methuselah normally receives **4 transfers** at the start of their influence phase [src-001 p. 35].
- Transfers, like master phase actions, are **not represented by counters and cannot be saved** for later use [src-001 p. 35].

## First-Three-Turns Ramp-Up
To balance going-first advantage, the first three turns have reduced transfers for the opening Methuselahs [src-001 p. 35]:

| Turn-order seat | 1st influence phase transfers |
|-----------------|------------------------------|
| 1st Methuselah  | **1** |
| 2nd Methuselah  | **2** |
| 3rd Methuselah  | **3** |
| 4th Methuselah onward | **4** (standard) |

From each Methuselah's **second** influence phase onward, they receive the standard **4** transfers.

## How Transfers Are Spent
During your influence phase you may spend transfers as follows [src-001 p. 36]:

- **1 transfer** → move **1 pool** counter from your pool to a vampire in your uncontrolled region.
- **2 transfers** → move **1 blood** counter from a vampire in your uncontrolled region back to your pool.
- **4 transfers** + **burn 1 pool** → move a vampire from your **crypt** to your **uncontrolled region** (influence a new vampire).

## Moving to Ready
At any time during this phase, if an uncontrolled vampire has at least as many blood counters as their capacity, you can move them **face up** to the **ready** region, **unlocked** [src-001 p. 36]:
- Counters are kept on them to represent blood.
- Counters in excess of capacity drain immediately (as usual).
- If the vampire's card grants **additional transfers**, those **cannot be used this turn** — transfers are gained at the start of the phase [src-001 p. 36].

## Advanced Vampires — Merge
An **advanced** vampire is a type of crypt card. Advanced cards have a special icon (a small bar under the clan icon). An advanced card is a vampire card in all respects, so it can be influenced normally [src-001 p. 37].

### Merging
If you already control the advanced **or** the associated regular "base" vampire, and the **other version** is in your uncontrolled region, you can [src-001 p. 37]:
- Spend **4 transfers** + **burn 1 pool** to move the uncontrolled version to merge with the controlled card — place the advanced card **on top** of the base card.
- The two cards effectively become a **single** merged vampire.

### Merge Mechanics
- Any counters or cards (if any) on the vampire coming from uncontrolled are **burned**; the in-play vampire keeps their counters/cards [src-001 p. 37].
- Treated as a single vampire card, even if sent to uncontrolled later, until the vampire is burned.
- Effects already targeting the vampire now target the merged version.

### Merged Card Text
- The **base card's text** still applies.
- The rest of the base card is **ignored** (capacity, Disciplines, etc.).
- The **advanced card applies in full**.
- On conflict (e.g., different sect), the **advanced card has precedence**.
- Some merged cards have an additional effect that **only applies when merged** — identified by a merged icon in the card text [src-001 p. 37].

### Contesting Merged Copies
The advanced vampire (merged or not) **contests** other copies of the same vampire — advanced or not — in play, as usual [src-001 p. 37].

## Example (from rulebook, p. 36)
Nora has 4 transfers and only 2 pool. She has a 6-blood stockpile on Alexa Draper (capacity 8) and wants to bring her out. She can't spend her 2 pool to put Draper in play — that would oust her. She previously invested 2 blood in Sybren van Oosten (capacity 7). She spends 2 transfers to pull 1 of those 2 back to her pool (now she has 3 pool and 2 transfers left). Then she spends her remaining 2 transfers to move 2 of the 3 pool counters onto Draper, filling her capacity of 8 — and moves Draper to the ready region.

## Scarce Trait Interaction
When moving a **[[traits#scarce|scarce]]** vampire from uncontrolled to ready during your influence phase, you must burn **3 pool** for each vampire of the **same clan** you already control [src-001 p. 41].

## Common Mistakes
- Saving unused transfers — you cannot.
- Counting opening-turn transfers wrong (1/2/3 for first three Methuselahs on **their first** influence phase only).
- Using new transfer capacity granted by a vampire on the same turn they come into play — cannot.
- Allowing merge without burning the 1 pool.
- Forgetting to burn scarce penalty pool.
