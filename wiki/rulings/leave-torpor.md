# Leave Torpor

## Summary
A self-action by a vampire **in torpor** to move back to the ready region. Pays 2 blood by default. If blocked by a vampire, the blocker gets a chance to **diablerise** instead of a normal combat [src-001].

## Action Profile [src-001]
- **Who can leave torpor:** a vampire in torpor.
- **Default cost:** 2 blood.
- **Default target:** none. **Undirected** action.
- **Default stealth:** +1.

## Effect
- On success: the acting vampire moves to the **ready region**. A vampire leaving torpor is **no longer wounded**.
- On block (blocker is a vampire): **no combat** (vampires in torpor cannot enter combat). The blocking vampire may opt to **diablerise** the acting vampire. If they decline, the action simply fails — the cost is not paid, and the acting vampire remains in torpor.
- On block (blocker is an ally): the action fails. No combat, no diablerie, no cost paid.

## Why This Matters
- A vampire leaving torpor who gets blocked is exposing themselves to diablerie. The blocker decides whether to take the opportunity.
- If the block fails (e.g., via stealth escalation), diablerie is off the table — the action resolves normally.

## Common Cards Involved
- Wake effects and stealth cards can help a vampire leave torpor unopposed.
- See [[rescue-from-torpor]] for the alternate path where another ready vampire fetches them.

## Common Mistakes
- Assuming combat follows a blocked leave-torpor — it doesn't. Vampires in torpor cannot enter combat.
- Forgetting that the 2-blood cost is only paid on **success**, like any action cost.
- Forgetting to mend the "wounded" status when leaving torpor succeeds.
