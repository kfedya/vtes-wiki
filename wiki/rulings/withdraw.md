# Withdrawing from the Game

## Summary
**Withdraw** is an optional way to end participation in the game. A Methuselah who has **exhausted their library** and **begins their turn with less than a full hand** may announce intent to withdraw during their unlock phase. If they avoid combat, blood loss, and pool loss until their next unlock phase, they successfully withdraw and gain **1 victory point** [src-001 p. 38 Advanced].

## Preconditions
A Methuselah may attempt to withdraw when **both** of these are true [src-001 p. 38]:
- Their **library is exhausted**.
- They **begin their turn with less than a full hand**.

## Declaration
Intent to withdraw must be **announced during the unlock phase** of the turn in which you attempt to withdraw [src-001 p. 38].

## Conditions for Success
For the withdrawal to succeed, from the moment of declaration until the **next unlock phase**, all of the following must hold [src-001 p. 38]:
- **None of your minions enter combat.**
- **None of your minions lose (or spend) any blood.**
- **You do not lose (or spend) any pool.**

If you meet all conditions when your next unlock phase would start, you successfully withdraw.

## Failure
The withdrawal **fails** if you **lose a single blood or pool counter**, even if you also gain enough to make up for the loss [src-001 p. 38].

## Reward
On success, you receive **1 victory point** added to any victory points already gained [src-001 p. 38].

- Your **predator does not get** 1 victory point or any pool for your withdrawal [src-001 p. 38].

## Tournament Override — half VP
In **VEKN-sanctioned tournament play** the reward is reduced to **0.5 VP** for a successful withdrawal, not the basic-rules 1 VP [src-032 §3.7.2]. Everything else (preconditions, declaration, success conditions, predator gets nothing) is unchanged. See [[../tournament/scoring]].

This makes withdrawing strictly worse than surviving as the **sole** survivor (1 VP) and equal to surviving when at least one other player is still at the table (also 0.5 VP). Withdraw is therefore not a tactic to chase VP under tournament scoring — it's an exit ramp when you can no longer interact with the game.

## Comparison to Ouster
- Ouster: predator gets 1 VP + 6 pool (usually) — see [[ending-the-game]].
- Withdrawal (basic rules): only the withdrawing Methuselah gets 1 VP; predator gets nothing.
- Withdrawal (tournament): only the withdrawing Methuselah gets 0.5 VP; predator still gets nothing [src-032 §3.7.2].

## Common Mistakes
- Declaring intent in a phase other than unlock.
- Spending pool on master phase actions after declaring.
- Losing 1 blood on a bleed action with a minion, ignoring the "no blood loss" rule.
- Assuming a net-zero gain/loss on blood or pool still counts as "no loss" — any **single** loss fails the attempt.
- Expecting the predator to still get 1 VP for the withdrawal — they don't.
