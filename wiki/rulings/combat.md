# Combat

## Summary
Combat happens when a minion blocks the action of a ready minion (see [[block-resolution]]) or when a card causes it. Only **ready** minions may fight, and you cannot enter combat with your own minions. Combat unfolds as one or more rounds, each with seven steps; the **acting minion** always plays effects first at every step [src-001 pp. 28–29].

## Who Fights
- Only **ready** minions participate. Locked and unlocked status is irrelevant for combat itself: locked minions fight exactly like unlocked ones [src-001 p. 28].
- A minion never fights another minion under the same controller [src-001 p. 28].
- The two minions in a combat are **combatants**; each is the other's **opposing minion**.
- The only minion cards playable **during** combat are **combat cards** [src-001 p. 29]. Some combat cards are played by minions "not involved in the current combat" — those can come from **any** Methuselah's minions [src-001 p. 29 Advanced].

## Round Structure — the Seven Steps
Each round has the following steps in this exact order [src-001 p. 29]:

1. **Before Range** — play cards that must be played before range is chosen. See [[#1-before-range]].
2. **Determine Range** — use maneuvers to set close or long. See [[maneuver]].
3. **Before Strikes** — play cards that must be played after range is set but before strikes are chosen.
4. **Strike** — announce and resolve strikes. See [[strike]] and [[strike-effects]].
5. **Damage Resolution** — prevent, then mend. See [[damage-resolution]].
6. **Press** — continue or end combat. See [[press]].
7. **End of Round** — play end-of-round effects; this step happens even if combat ends prematurely [src-001 p. 33].

The acting minion always has the first opportunity to use cards or effects before the opposing minion at every stage [src-001 p. 29].

### 1. Before Range
Some cards or effects are clearly stated to be played before range is determined. Effects that are played "before range is determined" must be played **before** the acting minion decides whether or not to play a maneuver [src-001 p. 29].

### 3. Before strikes
Some combat cards are only playable **after** range has been set but **before** strikes have been chosen [src-001 p. 30].

### 7. End of Round
Cards or effects usable only at end of round are played after press. This step still occurs if combat ended prematurely (e.g., a strike sent someone to torpor) [src-001 p. 33].

## Priority (the Impulse in Combat)
At every step the **acting minion** (the one who was performing the action, or the instigator of the combat) plays effects first; the opposing minion responds [src-001 p. 29]. If anyone plays an effect, the acting Methuselah gets the impulse back (see [[game-overview#Important terms]] / Impulse glossary) [src-001 p. 42].

## Early Termination
If during any step either combatant is no longer **ready** — for example, because one has taken too much damage — the round and combat **end immediately**. This is true at any point, not just strike resolution [src-001 p. 30]. The end-of-round step still happens [src-001 p. 33].

## Retainers in Combat
- A retainer is **not harmed** in combat by default, unless its employer is burned [src-001 p. 33].
- An attacker may instead choose to target the opposing minion's **retainer** with a strike, **only at long range**, and must announce the target when the strike is announced [src-001 p. 33].
- Dodge does **not** protect retainers — the dodging minion is protected, but their retainer is not [src-001 p. 33].
- See [[damage-resolution#retainers-and-allies]] for life-counter handling.

## Ranged Rules Summary
- **Close** is the default range each round.
- A **maneuver** switches range. Ping-pong is allowed; a minion cannot play two maneuvers in a row [src-001 p. 29].
- Most strikes are close-range only. **Ranged** strikes, strikes doing **R** damage, and defensive strikes (dodge, combat ends) work at any range [src-001 p. 30].
- Full details: [[maneuver]].

## Strike Order (very short)
- **Combat ends** resolves first, before any other strike — even first strike [src-001 p. 33].
- **First strike** resolves before normal strikes; if both combatants strike with first strike, they resolve simultaneously [src-001 p. 34].
- Normal strikes resolve simultaneously [src-001 p. 30].
- **Additional strikes** happen after the normal pair; only minions with additional strikes play during them [src-001 pp. 30–31].
- Full details: [[strike]], [[strike-effects]].

## Important Edge Cases
- **Maneuver / press "only this round":** a maneuver from a card and a press granted by a card can only be used the round they're gained. They cannot be saved. See [Resist Earth's Grasp](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Resist%20Earth%27s%20Grasp) [src-001 p. 50].
- **Weapon maneuver commits to weapon strike:** if a minion uses a maneuver from a weapon or strike card, they are committed to that weapon/card as their strike. If that strike is cancelled (e.g., by Immortal Grapple), they **cannot strike at all** this round — they do not get to choose another strike. See [.44 Magnum](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=.44%20Magnum) [src-001 p. 47].
- **Immortal Grapple timing:** must wait until no more maneuvers are played before being played. [Immortal Grapple](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Immortal%20Grapple) [src-001 p. 49].
- **Roundhouse is a hand strike** — therefore it works with Immortal Grapple (which confines the round to hand strikes) [src-001 p. 50].
- **Hidden Strength** cannot be played to "cycle for press" when there is no damage to prevent [src-001 p. 49].
- **Freak Drive after blocked action** is played **after** combat; can even be played if the acting vampire is now in torpor, provided they have blood to pay [src-001 p. 49]. See also [[post-combat-effects]].
- **Deep Song at superior:** if action not blocked, target minion is treated as the acting minion in the resulting combat — they play cards first [src-001 p. 48].
- **The Barrens** — can be used at **any time, including in combat**, if you are in dire need of a more useful card [src-001 p. 47].
- **[[wiki/cards/theft-of-vitae|Theft of Vitae]]** — usable at any range; steal is not damage (can't be prevented); strike: combat ends resolves first (you lose the steal if combat ends); steal resolves before mend [src-001 p. 51].

## Common Mistakes
- Forgetting that combat ends resolves **before** first strike.
- Letting a dodge protect a retainer — it does not.
- Using a weapon maneuver and still trying to pick a non-weapon strike.
- Letting locked status stop a minion from fighting — it does not; only readiness matters.
- Allowing a minion to play two maneuvers (or two presses) in a row — they cancel and are prohibited.
