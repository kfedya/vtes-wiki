# Blood Hunt

## Summary
A **blood hunt** is vampiric society's penalty for diablerie. When a vampire commits [[diablerie]], an automatic and immediate referendum is called to decide whether a blood hunt is declared. If it passes, the diablerist is **burned**. The blood-hunt referendum is not an action, so it **cannot be blocked**, and **action modifiers and reaction cards cannot be played** during it [src-001 p. 35].

## Trigger
Immediately after a diablerie resolves — no player choice, no delay [src-001 p. 35].

## The Referendum
The blood-hunt referendum follows the usual three-step structure (see [[referendum]]) — terms, polling, resolve — with the following restrictions [src-001 p. 35]:

- **Not an action** — cannot be blocked.
- **Action modifiers** cannot be played.
- **Reaction cards** cannot be played.
- Otherwise handled like any other referendum. All standard voting rules and vote sources apply (titles, Edge-for-1-vote, political-action votes, etc. — see [[voting]]).

## Effect if Passed
The **diablerist is burned** immediately [src-001 p. 35].

## Ties and Failure
Tied referendums **fail** [src-001 p. 28 — Referendum]. A failed blood-hunt referendum has **no effect**; the diablerist is not burned.

## The Diablerie Itself Is Not Undone
Whether the blood hunt passes or fails, the diablerie's effects — blood drained, equipment taken, victim burned, any Discipline granted, any Red List trophy — are **already applied** [src-001 pp. 34–35]. The blood hunt only decides the diablerist's personal fate.

## Relationship to the Red Hunt via Cards
The automatic blood hunt described here is the **in-rule** penalty triggered by diablerie. Cards that call a blood hunt (e.g., political-action "blood hunt" cards) work via the normal political-action path: they **are** actions, can be blocked, etc. The restrictions above apply only to the **automatic** referendum triggered by an actual diablerie.

## Common Mistakes
- Letting a defender play a wake, reaction, or action-modifier card during the blood-hunt referendum — not allowed.
- Trying to block the blood-hunt referendum — not an action, cannot be blocked.
- Expecting a failed blood hunt to undo the diablerie — it doesn't.
- Forgetting to call the referendum at all after a successful diablerie.
