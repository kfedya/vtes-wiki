# Bleed

## Summary
A **bleed** is a directed action — "the efforts of your minion to undermine the power and pool of the target Methuselah" — that burns pool from the target. Any ready minion can bleed; a minion cannot perform **more than one bleed action per turn**, even if they unlock [src-001].

## Action Profile [src-001]
- **Who can bleed:** any ready minion (vampire or ally).
- **Default cost:** none.
- **Default target:** your prey. **Directed** action. Cards can redirect to another Methuselah. You can never bleed yourself.
- **Default stealth:** 0.
- **Default bleed amount:** 1 (every vampire has a base bleed amount of 1).

## Effect
If the action succeeds, the target Methuselah **burns pool equal to the bleed amount**.

**Edge gain:** if the action succeeds **and** the bleed amount is ≥ 1, the acting minion's controller takes the **Edge** from its current holder (or uncontrolled) [src-001].

> The bleed action is successful if it was not blocked. However, the bleed itself is considered successful only if the bleed amount is 1 or more. If it is reduced to 0 or less, it is not successful and you do not gain the Edge. [src-001 p. 46]

## The (limited) Rule for Bleed
> During a bleed action, an action modifier card cannot be played to increase the bleed if the bleed amount is already being increased by another action modifier card (unless any of them does not count against that limit). [src-001]

Cards that count against this limit are reminded with "(limited)" card text.

Exceptions exist: some cards (e.g., [Perfect Paragon](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Perfect%20Paragon) at superior, [Foreshadowing Destruction](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Foreshadowing%20Destruction) with lingering-only effect) do not count as increasing the bleed in some situations and can be played around a limited modifier.

## FAQ [src-001 pp. 46, 48–51]

**When can I play Conditioning / Aire of Elation / Govern the Unaligned to increase the bleed?**
> You must announce the action first, then play any effects that are "usable when the action is announced" before being able to play action modifiers to increase the bleed. But it is even better to wait until the target Methuselah has declined to block to increase the bleed.

**When can I reduce a bleed?**
Same window as increasing: any time after the action has been announced and after "as-announced" effects.

**When can I play Deflection / Telepathic Misdirection / Redirection (change the target)?**
If you are the target of a bleed, you **must first decline to block**. That gives the acting Methuselah one last chance to increase the bleed. Whether or not they do, you can then play a Deflection; the new target can in turn block or redirect [src-001 p. 46].

**If my redirected bleed is redirected *back* to me, can I block?**
Yes — a target change **reopens** the block window for the new target [src-001 p. 46, p. 26].

**Do I get the Edge if I bleed for 0?**
No. The action is "successful" (resolved unblocked), but the bleed itself is not successful and no Edge is transferred.

**A locked vampire under a wake effect (On the Qui Vive) — can they play Deflection?**
Yes, even at basic. Deflection at basic will lock them — but they are already locked, so the lock has no further effect — and redirect the bleed [src-001 p. 48].

## Common Cards Involved
- [Conditioning](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Conditioning) — Dominate modifier, "(limited)" bleed increase; see [Aire of Elation ruling](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Aire%20of%20Elation).
- [Deflection](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Deflection) — redirect the bleed; see also [[reaction]] and "change of target reopens blocks".
- [Telepathic Misdirection](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Telepathic%20Misdirection) — See Deflection.
- [Spying Mission](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Spying%20Mission) — superior playable only after the bleed is known to succeed for 1+ (i.e., after reductions/redirects resolve) [src-001 p. 51].
- [Foreshadowing Destruction](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Foreshadowing%20Destruction) — quirk: against a target with ≥ 10 pool it is "lingering" and does **not** count as increasing the bleed that action; a limited modifier can still be played [src-001 p. 49].

## Common Mistakes
- Playing a bleed modifier at announcement instead of after "as-announced" effects.
- Playing a second limited bleed modifier on top of a first one.
- Expecting the Edge from a 0-bleed "successful" action.
- Playing Deflection before declining to block (you must decline first).
