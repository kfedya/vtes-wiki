# Unlock Phase

## Summary
The first phase of a Methuselah's turn. They **unlock all their cards** (turn them back upright), then resolve any effects that happen during unlock, including the Edge gain and any contest payments [src-001].

## Step-by-Step Sequence
1. **Unlock all your cards.** Contested cards remain face-down and out of play — they do not unlock.
2. **Resolve "during your unlock phase" effects** in any order you choose:
   - **Edge** — if you hold the Edge, you may gain 1 pool from the blood bank.
   - **Contest payments** — pay 1 pool per contested unique card you are contesting, and 1 blood per vampire contesting a title (or yield — see below).
   - **Burn-option cards** — see Advanced below.
   - Card-specific unlock-phase triggers (Blood Doll, Vessel, Smiling Jack, Anarch Troublemaker, etc.).

## Contested Cards (Advanced)
Some cards are marked **unique** in card text, and every crypt card is inherently unique [src-001].

- If more than one unique card with the same name is in play, they are **all contested**: turned face-down and out of play for the duration of the contest.
- **Contest cost:** 1 pool per unlock phase, per contested unique card. Paid by the controller.
- **Yielding:** instead of paying, you may choose to yield. A yielded card is **burned**; any cards or counters on it are burned with it.
- When all other contesters yield, the remaining card is unlocked and turned face-up **on your next unlock phase** — the contest ends then, not instantly.

Deck-building: you cannot control more than one of a unique card at a time, and you cannot voluntarily contest with yourself. If an effect would force you to contest a card with yourself, you simply **burn the incoming copy** [src-001].

## Contested Titles (Advanced)
Some titles are unique — e.g., only one prince or baron of a given city (see [[game-overview]] for sect rules — full sect ingest is stage 4) [src-001].

- If more than one vampire claims the same unique title, the title is **contested**. Both vampires are treated as having **no title** during the contest, but they remain in play and can act and block normally.
- **Contest cost:** 1 blood per unlock phase, paid by the vampire. Only **ready** vampires can contest — vampires in torpor must yield.
- If a contesting vampire cannot pay, they are forced to yield.
- **Yielding:** the vampire loses the benefits of the title for the rest of the game. The title does not re-transfer if they later re-claim it — benefits are gone permanently.
- When all other contesters yield, the remaining vampire acquires the title on their next unlock phase.

## Burn Option (Advanced)
Some cards carry a **burn option** icon. A Methuselah may discard-and-replace such a card from their hand during **any** Methuselah's unlock phase — but only if they do not control a minion who meets the card's requirements **and** is a legal target for it [src-001].

- **Limit:** one burn-option discard per Methuselah per unlock phase.
- The discarded card is replaced per normal replacement rules (see [[library#Drawing Cards]]).

## Common Cards Involved
- [Blood Doll](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Blood%20Doll) — moves blood during unlock.
- [Vessel](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Vessel) — moves blood once per unlock phase (per the "During X, do Y" template).
- [Smiling Jack, The Anarch](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Smiling%20Jack,%20The%20Anarch) — **mandatory** 1 pool to the card during unlock, even if it ousts you [src-001 p. 50].
- [Anarch Troublemaker](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Anarch%20Troublemaker) — play during master phase; effect triggers next turn [src-001 p. 47].

## Common Mistakes
- Paying contest cost *before* unlocking (order doesn't matter mechanically, but contested cards stay face-down and are not "unlocked").
- Forgetting that the contest-end vampire/card only surfaces on **your next** unlock phase, not immediately when others yield.
- Treating a yielded title as recoverable — it isn't; the benefit is lost permanently.
