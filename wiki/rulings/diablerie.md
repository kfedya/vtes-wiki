# Diablerie (Full Resolution)

## Summary
**Diablerie** is the act of burning a vampire in torpor by drinking their blood. Only **ready** vampires can commit it. The diablerist drains the victim, may take equipment, and — if the victim was older — may gain a Discipline. Diablerie triggers an automatic blood-hunt referendum; if it passes, the diablerist is burned. For the **action profile** (how you initiate diablerie against a torpor vampire), see [[diablerise-action]]. This page covers the **resolution** itself [src-001 p. 34].

## Prerequisites
- The diablerist must be a **ready** vampire.
- The victim must be a vampire in **torpor**.
- A vampire who is **Blood Cursed** cannot commit diablerie [src-001 p. 41].

## Resolution Steps
Diablerie resolves as a **single atomic unit**. No effects can be used to interrupt diablerie; effects may be played either **before** or **after**, as appropriate [src-001 p. 35].

1. **Drain blood.** All blood on the victim is moved to the diablerist. Blood in excess of their capacity drains off as normal [src-001 p. 34].
2. **Take equipment (optional).** The diablerist may take **any** equipment on the victim [src-001 p. 35].
3. **Burn the victim.** The victim is burned. Any cards and counters on them are also burned [src-001 p. 35].
4. **Discipline reward (advanced).** If the victim had a **higher capacity** than the diablerist, the diablerist can be given a Discipline: their controller goes through their library, ash heap, and hand for a **master Discipline card**, puts it on the diablerist, then shuffles their library / draws back up to hand size as necessary [src-001 p. 35].
   - If the Discipline card increases the diablerist's capacity, the diablerie does **not** give 1 blood to fill the new capacity [src-001 p. 35].
5. **Red List trophies (if applicable).** If the victim was Red List, the diablerist may receive trophies (see the [Red List](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/) trait in [[traits]]) [src-001 p. 35].

## Immediate Aftermath: Blood-Hunt Referendum
As soon as diablerie resolves, a **blood-hunt referendum** is **automatically and immediately** conducted. See [[blood-hunt]] for the full mechanics [src-001 p. 35].

## Destruction by Aggravated Damage Is Not Diablerie
Destruction of a wounded vampire by aggravated damage does **not** constitute diablerie [src-001 p. 32]. So no Discipline reward, no trophy, no automatic blood hunt.

## Interaction with Leave Torpor
If a vampire attempts [[leave-torpor]] and is **blocked by a vampire**, the blocker gets a **diablerie opportunity** — not combat [src-001 p. 24 (see leave-torpor)].

## Who Holds the Impulse During Diablerie
Because diablerie resolves as a single unit, no cards or effects can interrupt the 5-step resolution above. Players can play effects before the diablerie begins or after it completes [src-001 p. 35].

## Common Mistakes
- Letting an opponent play a cancellation/prevention card between the blood drain and the burn — diablerie is atomic.
- Granting a Discipline when the victim wasn't **older** (higher capacity) than the diablerist.
- Granting 1 bonus blood to fill capacity when the Discipline raised the diablerist's capacity.
- Counting an aggravated-damage burn as diablerie — it isn't.
- Forgetting the automatic blood-hunt referendum after resolution.
