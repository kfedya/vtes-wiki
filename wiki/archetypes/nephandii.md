# Nephandii

Sabbat **allies-swarm** archetype using [Antonio d'Erlette](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/crypt/index.html?card=Antonio%20d%27Erlette) (Tremere antitribu) and his Sabbat-gated ability — spend 4 transfers (from influence phase) to put a Mage ally in play directly from library/hand. Repeated every turn, the deck assembles 4–6 [Nephandus](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Nephandus) (Mage ally, 2 life, 1 bleed, strike 1R damage, 1 optional press) and bleeds the prey to death via multiple cheap allies packing [Target Vitals](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Target%20Vitals) × 8. Codex Top Tier [src-014].

Canonical decklist: **Tomasz Kloczko**, English NC 2024, 2024-11-09, TWDA id 11521 [src-014].

## Core idea

1. **Influence [Antonio d'Erlette](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/crypt/index.html?card=Antonio%20d%27Erlette) fast.** Cap 5, group 4 Tremere antitribu Sabbat. Every ready Antonio can spend 4 transfers during your influence phase to put a Mage ally (Nephandus primarily) into play.
2. **Swarm Nephandus.** 10 copies in the library; the ally is cheap, resilient (2 life), and comes with a 1R damage strike + 1 optional press per combat.
3. **Amp bleeds with [Target Vitals](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Target%20Vitals) × 8.** Combat card — "Combat ends after this action. If this vampire's strike did any damage this round, the opposing minion takes +2 damage unpreventable." Nephandus can play Target Vitals (the card doesn't require discipline; any Mage / minion can play it).
4. **Economy stack.** [Liquidation](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Liquidation) × 4 (burn an out-of-play crypt for 3 pool), [Ashur Tablets](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Ashur%20Tablets) × 9 (3-copy triggers 13-card rebuild), [The Parthenon](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=The%20Parthenon) × 4 (location tap engine), [The Shard, London](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=The%20Shard%2C%20London) × 2 (cost reduction), [Dreams of the Sphinx](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Dreams%20of%20the%20Sphinx) × 3.
5. **Stealth allies into range.** [Mirror Walk](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Mirror%20Walk) × 4 — THA stealth / redirect. Allies play Mirror Walk to stealth-bleed or to redirect incoming combat.
6. **Defence.** [Deflection](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Deflection) × 7 (the only bleed defence), [Delaying Tactics](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Delaying%20Tactics) × 2 (anti-vote), [On the Qui Vive](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=On%20the%20Qui%20Vive) × 4 (wake/unlock).
7. **Events.** [The Unmasking](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=The%20Unmasking) × 3 — Sabbat / allies-friendly event; [FBI Special Affairs Division](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=FBI%20Special%20Affairs%20Division) × 1 — anti-ally-removal from opponents.
8. **Opportunistic rush.** [Haven Uncovered](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Haven%20Uncovered) × 2, [Frontal Assault](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Frontal%20Assault) × 1 — force combat on a specific minion.
9. **Extras.** [Secure Haven](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Secure%20Haven) × 2 (protects Antonio from redirected bleeds/combats); [Tupdog](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/crypt/index.html?card=Tupdog) × 3 in crypt dual-purpose — blocker + metagame decoy (looks like a Tupdog-rush deck).
10. **Carlton Van Wyk** × 1 (Ally — hunter) — insurance against diablerie-predators.

## Key disciplines and clans

- **Clan:** Tremere antitribu (Antonio, Ember Wright, Keith Moody, Saiz) + Gargoyle (Tupdog) minor splash. Crypt is overwhelmingly Antonio × 6.
- **Disciplines on Antonio:** `AUS/DOM/THA` (legacy Tremere antitribu signatures).
- **Sect:** Sabbat — Antonio's ability is Sabbat-gated; Liquidation and the alliance decks reference Sabbat archetypally.

## Support / tech

### Ally engine
Antonio's ability replaces an entire influence phase (4 transfers) with a free Mage ally in play. Nephandus has 2 life = survives most single-strike combats. With 4+ Nephandus ready, each turn delivers multiple bleeds.

### Target Vitals × 8
Nephandus strike = 1R damage; Target Vitals triggers the +2 damage rider and then ends combat. Effective against blockers: survive the first strike, deal 3 damage, exit combat — similar role to a ranged-combat strike but via an ally.

### Economy
Massive 30-master pile justified by Ashur Tablets rebuild + Liquidation's crypt-burn-for-pool. Parthenon × 4 redundancy protects against Pentex.

### Defence
Deflection × 7 is the *only* bleed-defence. No combat defence on vampires — Antonio relies on Secure Haven (protects him as a location). Allies take incoming combat.

## Variants

- **Darby Keeney** — streamlined variant dropping some defensive cards for faster ally count.
- **Martin Weinmayer "Bounce"** — adds more Auspex intercept/bounce for bleed-heavy metas.

## Weaknesses

1. **Ally-hate tech.** [Pentex™ Subversion](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Pentex%E2%84%A2%20Subversion) on Nephandus, [Horrific Countenance](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Horrific%20Countenance) / [Anarch Troublemaker](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Anarch%20Troublemaker) hurt the pace; a well-timed [Edge Explosion](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Edge%20Explosion) kills multiple allies.
2. **Archon Investigation.** Antonio risking diablerie triggers Archon — Secure Haven × 2 partial protection.
3. **Fast oust decks.** Hesha's Emporium wins before the 4-Nephandus tempo comes online.
4. **Heavy intercept walls.** Bleed with Nephandus is 1 base + Mirror Walk stealth — a +3 intercept wall (Gangrel Thing) blocks cleanly.
5. **Only Mage allies qualify.** Antonio's ability is narrow — only Mages (Nephandus and a handful of others) are valid targets. Reduced versatility.
