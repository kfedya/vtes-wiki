# Haqim Royalty

Banu Haqim **Camarilla toolbox** archetype: ranged-combat specialists with Prince/Justicar crypt running the **Haqim's Law: Retribution** payload. The deck's 38-card combat module doubles as a bleed amplifier — each combat card discarded during a bleed adds +1 to that bleed — while [Second Tradition: Domain](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Second%20Tradition%3A%20Domain) × 9 supplies the only real defence (requires Prince or Justicar). Codex classifies it as one of 4 ⭐ "Top Dog" current-meta archetypes [src-006].

Canonical decklist: **Gyula Erdős**, "Beauty challenge" 2024-11-03, TWDA id 11674 [src-006]. Other referenced builds on the Codex article: **Luís Otávio Franco Martins** (TWDA id 11167, Biothaumaturgic Experiment splash), **Darby Keeney** (id 11470, more political with Alamut / The Black Throne), **Pauli Tuomiluoma** (id 10919, swarmier via The Embrace / Third Tradition: Progeny), **Juan Luis Mejías Luna** (id 10636, similar swarm).

## Core idea

1. **Seat a cap-6+ titled Banu Haqim early.** Crypt leans on [Kassandra Tassaki](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/crypt/index.html?card=Kassandra%20Tassaki) (×3 — cap 6, Camarilla Prince of Thessaloniki) and [Kasim Bayar](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/crypt/index.html?card=Kasim%20Bayar) (×2 — cap 9, Camarilla Assamite Justicar with combat-mode politics discard). Titles unlock [Second Tradition: Domain](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Second%20Tradition%3A%20Domain).
2. **Drop [Haqim's Law: Retribution](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Haqim%27s%20Law%3A%20Retribution)** (Master, 2 copies base — often increased). Every subsequent bleed action can discard one combat card for +1 bleed.
3. **Bloat while posturing.** [Villein](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Villein) × 5 drains blood-to-pool on the cap-7/8 hosts; [Anathema](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Anathema) and [Priority Contract](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Priority%20Contract) (unique trifle) each score a near-guaranteed +3 pool from the prey. [Ashur Tablets](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Ashur%20Tablets) × 9 + [Infernal Pursuit](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Infernal%20Pursuit) × 5 set up the 3-copy tutor trigger for a 13-card rebuild.
4. **Bleed with Retribution fuel.** Each bleed action pitches a combat card for +1 bleed. Modifiers like [Resist Earth's Grasp](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Resist%20Earth%27s%20Grasp) (action-mod / combat) can discard to Retribution when the prey doesn't block, directly trading defensive tempo for bleed size. [Swallowed by the Night](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Swallowed%20by%20the%20Night) × 1 pulls double duty as stealth or combat-card feed.
5. **Eat blocks with the brutal combat module.** [Hunger of Marduk](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Hunger%20of%20Marduk) × 9 is ranged THA blood-theft (2 at superior); coupled with additional strikes from [Quickness](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Quickness) × 4 / [Blur](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Blur) × 3 / [Psyche!](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Psyche%21) × 5 it consistently drains the opposing vampire. [Pursuit](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Pursuit) × 7 and [Infernal Pursuit](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Infernal%20Pursuit) × 5 lock in the CEL press chain and draw support.
6. **Maneuver into range.** THA is ranged; range control is non-negotiable to dodge [Immortal Grapple](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Immortal%20Grapple) and [Claws of the Dead](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Claws%20of%20the%20Dead). [Rego Motum](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Rego%20Motum) × 2 and [Sideslip](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Sideslip) × 1 handle this alongside printed maneuvers on Hunger of Marduk.
7. **Rush defensively.** [Hunter's Mark](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Hunter%27s%20Mark) × 6 (CEL/THA rush; CEL/THA superior also kills the opposing strike that round) works anywhere on the table — used primarily to slow down an aggressive predator, since Priority Contract can only be pointed forward.

## Key disciplines and clans

- **Clan:** Banu Haqim (V5 rebrand of Assamite).
- **Disciplines:** `[THA]` Blood Sorcery (Hunger of Marduk, Rego Motum, Haqim's Law bleed interaction); `[CEL]` Celerity (Pursuit family, additional-strike package, Blur, Quickness); `[OBF]` Obfuscate (Swallowed by the Night stealth). Inferior splashes throughout the combat module.
- **Sect:** Camarilla — every titled vampire in the crypt is a Camarilla Prince or Justicar, and [Second Tradition: Domain](https://codex-of-the-damned.org/en/card-search/library/index.html?card=Second%20Tradition%3A%20Domain) requires it.
- **Title votes:** Justicar (Kasim Bayar) = 3 votes; Prince (Kassandra Tassaki, Oluwafunmilayo) = 2 votes. With 5 crypt slots titled the deck has innate vote power even without a vote payload.

## Support / tech

### Bloat stack
1. **Villein × 5** — 3 blood → 3 pool per copy. The cap-7/8/9 hosts can take repeated Villeins.
2. **Anathema × 1** — political action, +3 pool regardless of success through Domain-backed intercept.
3. **Priority Contract × 2** — unique trifle; near-guaranteed +3 pool if pointed at the prey's threat vampire. Also works if the prey gets ousted (the attached vampire leaves the game).
4. **The Parthenon × 3** — Camarilla-titled vampire tap engine.
5. **Ashur Tablets × 9** — bulk-drawer; with Infernal Pursuit's mid-combat cycle and Dreams of the Sphinx, the 3rd-copy trigger arrives on turn 6–8 for a 13-card rebuild.

### Defence
- **Second Tradition: Domain × 9** — only viable block card. Requires Prince or Justicar — unlocks any locked titled blocker for +2 intercept. Note: **this is the *only* real defence** (no bounce; no printed intercept elsewhere). Wide stealth predators can crack this wall.

### Combat
- **Ranged-strike core:** Hunger of Marduk × 9 + maneuver support.
- **Additional-strike package:** Pursuit × 7 (dodge+strike), Quickness × 4 (+1 strike), Blur × 3 (+1 strike, Celerity), Psyche! × 5 (press + strike).
- **Range-control:** Infernal Pursuit × 5 (press + cycle), Rego Motum × 2, Sideslip × 1.
- **Rush:** Hunter's Mark × 6 (CEL/THA enter-combat; at superior, kills opposing strike once).

### Event / lockouts
- **Rumors of Gehenna × 1** — event; shuts down the prey's best cards.

## Weaknesses

1. **Light defence.** Second Tradition: Domain × 9 is the only block line. Above 2 printed intercept is rare — well-stealthed vote and bleed predators (Malk' 22, Ravnos Bonds, The unnamed, Platinum Revelation) crash through it.
2. **No bounce.** Every bleed lands on pool. The deck compensates with bloat, but against 4-bleeds + multiple stealth decks the pool line gets shaky even with Villein + Anathema + Priority Contract stacked.
3. **Slow to come online.** Cap-6/7/8 hosts mean 2–3 turn influence; meanwhile Ashur Tablets × 9 slow down draw until the 3rd copy triggers. Hyper-fast predators (Hesha's Emporium) can oust before the toolbox matures.
4. **Priority Contract is forward-only.** Defensive rush requires Hunter's Mark, which is single-copy pressure — not reusable like Priority Contract.
5. **Combat-card hand commitment.** Retribution pays off only when a combat card is in hand; every Retribution bleed competes with the defensive combat you might need that turn.
